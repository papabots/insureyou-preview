What Is Critical Illness Insurance?

Often called “Trauma Insurance,” Critical Illness Insurance gives you a lump sum if you’re diagnosed with, or suffer from, a serious medical condition like cancer, a heart attack, or stroke—usually with a specified level of severity. Most of Australia’s top insurers cover around 50 conditions.

These conditions can be life-changing, costly, or both—and sometimes private health insurance or the public system doesn’t cover everything. This could include things like major head injuries, severe burns, coma, or diagnoses such as Multiple Sclerosis, Parkinson’s, Muscular Dystrophy, brain tumours, and more.

For the full list of conditions covered, our brokers can guide you and help compare options across different insurers, so you can choose the policy that fits your needs.

I’ve Got Private Health—Why Do I Still Need Critical Illness Insurance?

Private health insurance is great for covering treatment costs, but Critical Illness Insurance works differently—it gives you a lump sum straight into your nominated bank account, usually tax-free, that you can use however you need. That could mean paying for medical and rehab costs, replacing lost income, giving your family time to support you, or covering other related expenses.

Think of it this way: Private Health Insurance and Critical Illness Insurance work hand-in-hand. One handles the bills, the other gives you flexibility and peace of mind, so you have the funds to get the care and support you need if a serious illness strikes.
