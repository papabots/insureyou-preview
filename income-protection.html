What is Income Protection Insurance?

Income Protection Insurance helps cover your income if you’re unable to work because of an illness, injury, or accident. It provides a regular monthly payment after a waiting period you choose, and continues until you’re able to return to work or until the benefit period ends.

Unless your policy says otherwise, you’re generally covered for any condition—physical or mental—that stops you from working.

Most policies can replace up to 70% of your pre-tax income, with some offering up to 90% for the first six months of a claim.

You can also choose options that continue your superannuation contributions, helping protect your long-term financial future if you’re off work for an extended time.

How Monthly Benefits Actually Work

If something stops you from working the way you normally do, income protection helps keep your income ticking along. Even if you’re able to go back part-time or take on lighter duties, the benefit can help cover the gap while you recover.

You’ll also decide how long payments could last. Some people choose short-term cover for peace of mind, while others want longer protection that can run right through to retirement age. What’s available depends on factors like your job, age, and health history.

There’s a waiting period before payments begin. This is simply the time between when you’re unable to work and when your benefit starts. Once that period ends, payments build up day by day and are paid monthly. Choosing a longer wait can sometimes help reduce the cost of cover.

The cost of income protection is often tax-deductible, which makes it a practical way to protect your income. In many cases, premiums can also be paid through your super fund, including industry or employer super accounts.
