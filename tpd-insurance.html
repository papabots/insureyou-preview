The Definition Matters

This is the most important box you will tick. The definition you choose decides how sick you have to be to actually get paid.

1. Own Occupation (The Gold Standard)

If you can't do your job, you get paid. This is the best protection. For example, a surgeon who loses a finger receives the full payout‚Äîeven if they could do a different, lower-paying job.

2. Any Occupation (The Basic Option)

If you can do any job, you might get nothing. Standard Super cover only pays if you cannot do any work you are reasonably suited for. Cheaper, but harder to claim.

3. Non-Working (Home Duties)

For stay-at-home parents. Covers you if physically unable to perform daily tasks like cooking, cleaning, or showering‚Äîor suffer a major loss of limbs or sight.
